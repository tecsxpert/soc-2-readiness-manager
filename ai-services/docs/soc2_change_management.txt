Change Management in SOC 2 Compliance

Change management is a critical control area in SOC 2 compliance that
governs how organizations plan, approve, implement, and review changes
to their systems, applications, and infrastructure. Effective change
management ensures that changes are made in a controlled and systematic
manner to minimize the risk of disruptions to services and security.

A formal change management process requires organizations to document
all proposed changes, including the purpose, scope, and potential
impact of each change. Changes must be reviewed and approved by
authorized personnel before implementation. This approval process
ensures that changes are properly evaluated for potential risks and
that appropriate testing has been conducted.

Organizations must implement procedures for testing changes in a
non-production environment before deploying them to production systems.
Testing should verify that changes function as intended and do not
introduce new vulnerabilities or break existing functionality. Change
rollback procedures must also be documented and tested to ensure
systems can be restored if a change causes unexpected issues.

Emergency change procedures must be established for urgent changes that
cannot follow the standard change management process. These procedures
should still require appropriate authorization and documentation, even
if the approval process is expedited. All emergency changes must be
reviewed after implementation to ensure they were necessary and
properly executed.

Auditors assess change management controls by reviewing change request
records, approval workflows, testing documentation, and deployment
logs. They verify that all changes have been properly authorized and
tested before implementation and that rollback procedures are in place.
