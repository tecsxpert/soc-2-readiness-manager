Business Continuity in SOC 2 Compliance

Business continuity management is a critical control area in SOC 2
compliance that ensures organizations can maintain or quickly resume
critical operations following a disruptive event. Effective business
continuity planning helps organizations minimize the impact of
disruptions on their customers, stakeholders, and business operations.

Organizations must develop a comprehensive business continuity plan
that identifies critical business processes and the resources required
to support them. The plan should define recovery time objectives and
recovery point objectives for each critical process, establishing
clear targets for how quickly operations must be restored and how
much data loss is acceptable in the event of a disruption.

Business impact analysis is a fundamental component of business
continuity planning. Organizations must assess the potential impact
of various disruption scenarios on their operations, revenue, and
reputation. This analysis helps prioritize recovery efforts and
allocate resources to the most critical business functions and systems.

Regular testing and exercising of business continuity plans is
essential to ensure their effectiveness. Organizations should conduct
tabletop exercises, simulation tests, and full recovery tests to
validate their plans and identify areas for improvement. Test results
must be documented and used to update plans and address identified
gaps and weaknesses.

Auditors assess business continuity controls by reviewing business
continuity plans, business impact analyses, and test records. They
verify that organizations have developed comprehensive plans that
address their critical business processes and that plans are regularly
tested and updated. Evidence of management support and employee
awareness of business continuity procedures is also required during
SOC 2 audits.
