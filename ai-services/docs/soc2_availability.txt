Availability in SOC 2 Compliance

Availability is one of the five Trust Service Criteria in SOC 2
compliance. It refers to the accessibility of systems, products, or
services as committed or agreed upon in service level agreements.
Organizations must ensure their systems are available and operational
to meet the needs of their customers and stakeholders.

System availability requires organizations to implement controls that
prevent and detect system failures, monitor system performance, and
restore services quickly when disruptions occur. This includes
establishing redundancy measures such as backup systems, failover
mechanisms, and load balancing to ensure continuous service delivery.

Organizations must maintain documented procedures for managing system
capacity and performance. This includes monitoring system resources
such as CPU usage, memory utilization, and storage capacity to prevent
performance degradation. Capacity planning should be conducted
regularly to ensure systems can handle current and future workloads.

Disaster recovery planning is a critical component of availability
management. Organizations must develop and test disaster recovery plans
that outline procedures for restoring systems and data in the event of
a major disruption. Recovery time objectives and recovery point
objectives must be defined and validated through regular testing.

Auditors assess availability controls by reviewing system monitoring
logs, incident records, and disaster recovery test results. They verify
that organizations have implemented appropriate controls to meet their
availability commitments and that systems are performing as expected.
Evidence of regular system maintenance, patch management, and capacity
planning activities is also reviewed during SOC 2 audits.
